# Card

<ComponentStatus stage="rc" responsive="yes" accessible="no" />

In Jobber a card is used to group related information and tasks to make things
easier to scan, and action upon. Cards also contain many optional sub-components
to provide extra structure in a predictable way.

## Simple Example

<Playground>
  <Card simple>
    <p>This is just…</p>
    <p>A simple card.</p>
  </Card>
</Playground>

<Playground>
  <Card accentColor="purple">
    <CardHeader>
      <h2>This is the exceptionally long card header.</h2>
      <p>This is the subtitle.</p>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent>
      <p>This is the card content.</p>
    </CardContent>

  </Card>
</Playground>

## ▸ `CardHeader`

The `CardHeader` defines the header section of a card. This is used to provide
supplementary information to the primary content of the card.

<Playground>
  <Card>
    <CardHeader>
      <h2>Sample Header</h2>
    </CardHeader>

    <CardContent />

  </Card>
</Playground>

<Props of={CardHeader} />

---

## ▸ `CardContent`

The `CardContent` defines the primary content of the card. If you only have
content you can pass `simple` to the base `Card` and ignore `CardContent` and
`CardHeader`.

<Playground>
  <Card>
    <CardHeader />

    <CardContent>
      <p>Card Content</p>
    </CardContent>

  </Card>
</Playground>
